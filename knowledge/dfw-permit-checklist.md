# DFW Metroplex Electrical Permit Requirements

Grizzly service area: Dallas, Garland, Mesquite, Plano, Richardson, Rowlett.
Texas adopted NEC 2020 statewide (effective 2023). Each city may have local amendments —
call the building department for the latest fee schedule; fees change frequently.

---

## What Triggers a Permit

A permit is required for any new electrical installation or alteration that is not like-for-like
replacement. In Texas, the general rule under NEC 2020 and TFER (Texas Electrical Safety Law):

### Always Requires a Permit
- **Panel upgrades** (any amperage increase or full panel replacement)
- **New circuits** (adding a circuit to an existing panel)
- **Service entrance work** (weatherhead, mast, meter base, SE cable)
- **New subpanels**
- **EV charger installs** (new dedicated circuit = new circuit permit)
- **Generator interlock or transfer switch installs**
- **Adding outlets, switches, or fixtures on a new circuit**
- **Any work in the meter socket** (must also coordinate with Oncor)
- **Pools and hot tubs** (separate permit, NEC Article 680)
- **Low-voltage systems that are part of new construction**

### Does NOT Require a Permit (Like-for-Like Replacements)
- Replacing an existing outlet or switch (same location, same circuit)
- Replacing a light fixture (same box, same circuit)
- Replacing a breaker in the same slot with same amperage rating
- Replacing a ceiling fan for a ceiling fan (existing outlet box rated for fan)
- Replacing a thermostat

> **Gray area**: adding AFCI/GFCI protection where it didn't exist before (e.g., upgrading a
> bedroom outlet to AFCI) is technically not like-for-like but most AHJs don't require a permit
> for single-device GFCI/AFCI upgrades. When in doubt, call the city.

## City-Specific Notes

### Dallas
- Online permit portal: **myDSD** (https://dallascityhall.com/departments/sustainabledevelopment/Pages/Online-Permits.aspx)
- Landlord/rental property: landlord must be named on permit; tenant cannot pull electrical permit
- Dallas requires licensed master electrician (TMLP) signature on residential permits
- Electrical inspection is coordinated through Development Services (DSD)

### Garland
- Permits: Garland Building Inspections (online via Citizenserve portal)
- Requires licensed electrician (TMLP or TELP depending on scope)

### Mesquite
- Permits: Community Development Department, Mesquite City Hall
- As of 2025: still using in-person/phone application for residential electrical

### Plano
- Permits: Plano Development Services (ePlans for commercial; simpler form for residential)
- Fast turnaround (often same-day for straightforward residential permits)

### Richardson
- Permits: Richardson Building & Development
- Residential electrical typically reviewed and approved within 1–2 business days

### Rowlett
- Permits: Rowlett Development Services
- Small city — call ahead, staff is limited; walk-ins accepted

## Oncor TDSP Coordination (Service Upgrades)

For any work on the **service entrance** (weatherhead through meter socket to main breaker):

1. **Pull the city electrical permit first.**
2. **Contact Oncor** to request a service upgrade work order.
   - Oncor requires a **Letter of Authorization** (LOA) from the licensed electrician
   - Oncor customer service: 1-888-313-4747 / oncor.com
3. **Typical lead time: 2–4 weeks** for Oncor scheduling. Do not promise a tight turnaround to the customer without checking with Oncor first.
4. **Oncor pulls the meter** before you begin work on the service entrance.
5. After rough-in and before energizing, **city rough-in inspection** must pass.
6. **Oncor re-installs meter** only after the city issues a green tag / approval.

> Quote panel upgrades with a line item for "Oncor coordination" and set realistic timeline expectations
> with the customer upfront (3–5 weeks total from permit to energized is common).

## Inspection Types

| Inspection | When It Occurs | Who Schedules |
|-----------|---------------|---------------|
| **Rough-in** | After wire is run, before drywall/cover | Electrician calls city AHJ |
| **Final** | After cover plates, panel labeled, all devices installed | Electrician calls city AHJ |
| **Service / Meter Inspection** | After panel install, before Oncor reinstalls meter | City AHJ + Oncor green tag |

Most cities allow scheduling inspections online or by phone, typically 24–48 hours in advance.

## Common Inspection Failures (What to Check Before Calling)

- **Missing bonding conductor** — grounding electrode conductor not properly bonded to water pipe, ground rod, or both (NEC 250.50, 250.53)
- **Neutral and ground not separated** in subpanels (they share a bar at main panel only; subpanel must have isolated neutral bar)
- **Open knockouts** in panel or junction boxes — all knockouts must be filled
- **Wrong wire gauge for breaker** — 14 AWG on a 20A breaker, 12 AWG on a 30A breaker, etc.
- **Improper grounding** — ground rods must be 8 ft, driven vertically, min 2 rods or one rod + concrete-encased electrode
- **No breaker directory/labeling** — panel directory must be filled out
- **Exposed splice outside a box** — all splices must be in covered junction boxes
- **AFCI/GFCI missing where required** — inspector will check all bedroom circuits and wet locations

## Bond Wire Sizing — 200A Service (NEC 250.102)

For a 200A service entrance:
- **Main bonding jumper** (neutral bar to panel enclosure): 4 AWG copper minimum (per NEC 250.102(C) Table)
- **Grounding electrode conductor** to ground rod: 6 AWG copper (per NEC 250.66)
- **Grounding electrode conductor** to water pipe or concrete-encased electrode: 4 AWG copper

> For aluminum SE cable (common on 200A service entrances): the neutral conductor in 2/0 AL SER
> cable is typically 1/0 AL, which meets NEC table requirements. Verify the neutral is bonded
> at the main panel only.

## Quick Reference: NEC 2020 AFCI/GFCI Requirements

| Location | AFCI Required | GFCI Required |
|----------|--------------|--------------|
| Bedrooms | Yes | No (unless within 6ft of sink) |
| Living rooms, family rooms | Yes | No |
| Kitchen countertop circuits | Yes | Yes |
| Bathrooms | Yes | Yes |
| Garage | No | Yes |
| Outdoor | No | Yes |
| Crawl space / unfinished basement | No | Yes |
| Laundry area | Yes | No (unless near sink) |
| Within 6 ft of any sink | No | Yes |
